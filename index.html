Who are we?

If These Lands Could Talk (ITLCT) is global network of change-makers, supporters, and allies who support Native and Indigenous communities to thrive authentically and on their own terms with digital technology both now and in the future.

We partner with communities and create aligned programming and events that amplify the voices, experiences, and excellence of Native and Indigenous populations throughout the world.

We believe in an authentic, responsible, inclusive, and sustainable digital future with positive social impact for Native and Indigenous communities across the globe.

A Global Ecosystem

Supporting authentic representation of Indigenous and Native communities throughout the world with digital technology and community engagement, but on their own terms.

ITLCT supports the rights to self-determination and agency with digital technology.

What We Do

  • Build Digital Fluency

    We support the enhancement of digital literacy to fluency transition. We assist and partner with communities to engage with digital technologies through education, community building, and networking.

  • Support community engagement

    We host community meetups in the metaverse and with digital technology. We partner with communities as they navigate questions around immersive and emerging technologies.

  • Curate digital technology programs

    We provide STEAM opportunities that are relevant to Native and Indigenous communities.

Land Acknowledgement

ITLCT operates predominantly through digital means. The Atlanta, Georgia area is the official home for ITLCT. Our leaders are located in the United States between the states of Georgia and Florida and so we wish to acknowledge both locations. We acknowledge that ITLCT leaders reside on the traditional homelands and territories of Florida consisting of but not limited to the Seminole Nation and other first peoples such as the Calusa, Tobobaga, Timucua, Apalachee, Miccosukee, Mascogo, Taíno, amongst others. ITLCT also acknowledge that we operate, and our leaders also reside on the traditional homelands and territories of Georgia consisting of the Cherokee Nation and other first peoples such as but not limited to the Catawba, Muscogee (Creek), Chickasaw, Shawnee, Oconee, Apalachicola, Chatot, amongst others.

The first peoples of Florida and Georgia who lived, worked, nurtured the land, and shared knowledge were involuntarily removed, faced difficult challenges and oppression, may still be seeking recognition and reconciliation in some cases, but today, they continue to shape their communities and the future in positive ways. ITLCT recognizes this and vows to be humble and respectful stewards of the lands, histories, and communities of Florida and Georgia's first people and other global tribes, nations, and communities as we engage in our work.
